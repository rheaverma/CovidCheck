Welcome to CovidCheck!

With the widespread outbreak of the Coronavirus, we know that these times are incredibly stressful. It's easy to find yourself outside of the loop of the vital information being passed around. In fact, sometimes, so much info is being thrown at you to the extent to which you don't know what to believe. CovidCheck provides an easy way to fact check your Corona news articles and compare statistics with daily updating information about the virus. CovidCheck also allows you to see predicted growth rates and how they change based on community social distancing practices. We also have essential tips to help minimize the spread as much as possible and put your mind at ease.

Tips

STAY HOME. SAVE LIVES.

Help stop coronavirus

Stay at Home

Refrain from leaving your house unless it is absolutely necessary. The best way to prevent yourself from contracting the virus is by distancing yourself from others.

Wash your hands

It is recommended by health care professionals to wash your hands for at least 20 seconds at a time. This is the best way to ensure that you don't become infected by the virus.

Wear a mask

If you must leave your house, wear a mask at all times to reduce the risk of contracting the virus. If you do not have a N95 mask, you can use a piece of cloth or a t-shirt with padding. If you are unable to get ahold of a N95 mask or create your own, ask around your community, as there are many people making masks for others.

Find a hobby

Self quarantine can be very difficult, especially since manys aspects of our daily life are taken away from us. For this reason finding a hobby such as playing an instrument, baking, reading, etc is a great way to keep yourself busy.

Donate to a local charity or food pantry

If you are able to donate to a local charity, please consider doing so. Many people are unable to provide for their families as a result of this pandemic and a donation of any amount would be really helpful.

Stop handling cash

While it's believed that the highest risk of acquiring coronavirus comes from person-to-person transmission, we do know that shared surfaces can harbor the virus. Play it safe by setting the cash aside for now and relying more on contactless payments.
